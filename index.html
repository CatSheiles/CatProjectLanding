Agile Development

The real risk with new products is not cost it's opportunity risk.

By prioritizing your product you are able to focus on what the product needs to be successful. Managing risk will become an exercise in not building too far ahead in the product roadmap with disciplined prioritization based on real-world feedback. Lay out a high level roadmap, key product attributes, resources required and a rough timeframe. Plan each sprint based on what are the highest value features you can build right now. Agile dev is a pay as you go approach, real value gets assigned to every feature in this model as items in the backlog compete to make the cut. Feature prioritization processees can get pretty ruthless and individual features become less precious. Only the best survive the environment.

Delighting customers and driving revenue, or adoption or whatever your key metric happens to be. Looking back over the twists and turns in your development cycle, you'll likely find fewer features on the trash heap and fewer rounds of iteration on the ones that survived.

The end result will be a product wherein you've maximized the efficiency of your capital investment and stayed focused on producing the best product possible.
